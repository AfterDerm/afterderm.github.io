Acne: basics & care

What it is: clogged pores and inflammation can lead to whiteheads, blackheads, and pimples. Gentle cleansing plus targeted actives help.

Educational only. Not a substitute for medical care. If acne is scarring, painful, or persistent, see a dermatologist.

Derm‑trusted over‑the‑counter picks

Routine tips

  • Wash once or twice daily with a gentle or salicylic acid cleanser.
  • Use a benzoyl‑peroxide product for inflamed spots.
  • Moisturize to protect your barrier; add sunscreen every morning.

Eczema: calm & protect

Eczema (atopic dermatitis) flares when the skin barrier is damaged. Daily moisturizers and trigger management are key.

Educational only. If infection, oozing, or widespread rash occurs, contact your clinician.

OTC moisturizers & relief

Psoriasis: soothe itch & hydrate

Thick, scaly plaques form due to rapid skin turnover. Hydration and anti‑itch ingredients can help between prescriptions.

Educational only. Talk to your dermatologist about prescription options and when to escalate treatment.

Rosacea: calm & protect

Rosacea often includes flushing and sensitivity. Gentle care + sun protection are essentials; avoid common triggers.

Educational only. If you have eye symptoms, swelling, or persistent burning, seek medical care.
